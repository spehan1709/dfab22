Laser-Cut accessories box

Creating the box

Ordinarily, I should have made at least a rough sketch of what I wanted to make, but instead I went straight Fusion 360 to start working on the box. I recommend that unless you are confident in a design in your head, you should start off a design with a sketch on pen and paper.

I used the parameter tool to define the sizes of the finger joints that hold the panels of wood together, which makes it much easier


Extensive use of the "cut" function from the combine tool allowed me to quickly make the shape of each piece of wood.

In the end, I came up with the final result.

Next, to cut out the part, for every piece, I clicked "create sketch" and clicked on the flat side of each part and stopped sketch. I then saved the sketch as a .SVG file to be put together on inkscape.

I then sent it to the lasercutter, got the wood and went home.

At home I laid out the pieces of wood to see what I was working with, and realised...

...I forgot to cut out a piece.


Please, when you go to cut the pieces of wood, make SURE that you actually cut out every piece of wood so that you don't have to go back for a single piece.


anyways, after cutting out the pieces, I glued everything together and the box was done.

:)
